Bio

I'm Ph.D. student at Stanford doing research in AI and machine learning, advised by Leonidas Guibas. I'm specifically interested in 3D content generation. I also enjoy making things in my spare time and having coffee with people.

I graduated from Columbia University with B.A.'s in Applied Math and Computer Science. During my undergrad years, I've had the pleasure of working with many wonderful faculty members both at Columbia and at Stanford. With Professor Eugene Wu at the Wu Lab, I investigated neural network interpretability. With Professor Peter Allen at the Columbia Robotics Lab I did some work on teleoperation using EEG data and autonomous navigation. I also did a project that made an attempt to counteract catastrophic forgetting through using adversarial examples with Professor Carl Vondrick. I led a project in Dr. Juan Carlos Nieble's group at Stanford on zero-shot inference through understanding object functionality in videos.
