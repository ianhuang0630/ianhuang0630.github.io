What Are Our Policies?

You've had a long time to finetune your parameters for all of our policies during the mulit-agent environment that is our friendship. Let's transfer that from simulation to the real world!

What's the best way to test policies? Obviously, put the agent in entirely new environments and see how they do. Do you know what we would do in crazy circumstances? Do you know what you would do? Find out on this quizlet (passwd: partyitupbirfdayboi). Go to 'flashcards'>'options' > select 'answer with: Definition', then 'flashcards' > select 'shuffle'. How well do you know us?

Log onto this chat, but don't put your name. We will do it at the same time as you. Instead of putting your actual name, put a string of numbers. We will do the same. This number will be the identifying number that we use later on.

You get to pick who you want to imitate first (we'll play a few rounds of this with different targets). The job of the the other two people is to figure out which person is real and which one is you, and identify you based on your username. These two will be asking you and your target questions. Talk like how your target would talk, respond to questions like how they would respond.

Some rules:

- No questions can be asked about technical, or domain specific knowledge that only your target would know. That would be unfair to you since Anna has too expansive of a vocabulary in the types of peanut butter.

- The two people who aren't vicitms of imitation must unanimously agree on which username belongs to you. Otherwise, you win.

- The total amount of time these two have to figure out the answer is 5 minutes. There will be a timer to keep track of time.

Decrypt This Enigma

The worst thing has happened on Thursday… No don’t worry -- it has nothing to do with your vim configuration. It’s Turing the plant. He is missing. Only his pot and parched soil remain. Turing himself is nowhere to be found. Worse yet, you (and Mo) weren’t there when it happened. You were out of town on a road trip to bathe in a remote waterfall. But you know for a fact that each of us was at your apartment for various reasons during the day. That’s right, one of us is the perpetrator. Your mission is to put on your detective hat and figure out which one of us did it.

Here are some things you should know…

Anna and you are in a fight (what’s new though, amirite) and she’s pissed. Apparently, you told her that Tama needed to be better behaved, and that “even your plants are more attuned to human instructions than Tama.” She’s still not over it and has suspended you from dinners. You better go apologize.

On Thursday, Amei was on call for her EMT shift. She did, however, recently get promoted to Crew Chief - and got everything luxurious that comes with it: a coveted new patch on her jacket, special swipe access, and an inflated ego (due to the now-higher chance of making it into Stanford med). She’s stressed about the job, and has been complaining about how much class she’s missing because of all the calls.

Ian, being the Californian boy that he is, is trying out LSD. He claims that it’s able to make him smarter than the typical Ph.D, but let’s not kid ourselves - he doesn’t need any LSD to be the brightest in the room. Downside, of course, is that he hallucinates when he’s high, and claims at some point, you stopped trying to convince him to switch to I-3. He has reported that the hallucinations are “trippy as fuck, yo.”

It’s also the beginning of classes. Seeing as it’s the last school year – Ian deferred his Ph.D. for a semester to spend an additional term at Columbia for his friends (:3) and to acclimate to the LSD usage – everyone decides to let loose and take the classes that they’ve always wanted to take. You can find the schedule here but you don’t know who’s taking what. However, here is a list of hints. You can assume that everyone goes to their classes, but if someone gets hurt Amei obviously needs to leave if she’s on call.

The day of the crime, Anna, Amei, and Ian all had their own reason for having to stop by your place. Because of your poor sense of reliability in people, you tasked Ian with watering Turing et al. while you were away. Anna wanted to stop by because she wants the jar of cinnamon she lent to you back (she has to make her small bowl of banana peanut butter honey baby-food). Amei wants to get her piano back after lending it to you to make music. What you do know is that Anna made a trip at 12pm-12:30pm, Amei made a trip at 5pm-5:30pm, and Ian made a trip at 3pm - 3:30pm.

Talking to the front desk lady, you know for a fact that she claims to have seen one of the girls return a second time that day, although she doesn’t remember anything else other than the fact that she was out on a smoke break after lunch, and that the girl was tall and nice.

You’re about to interrogate each of them via chat. But you only have 21 questions to do so. Here are some rules: - You cannot ask if we are lying - You cannot ask who killed the plant - You cannot ask which class a person is in. - There will be one lie told throughout the interrogations. It will be told by the culprit. All other statements are true. - To win the game, identify the criminal in 21 questions, and have the correct justification. What was the lie?
